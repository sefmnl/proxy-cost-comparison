## What private proxies actually are (no jargon)

A private proxy is an IP address assigned to you and only you. No shared usage. No neighbor scraping LinkedIn at full throttle and getting your IP banned by association. The "private" part is the whole point — you get exclusive access for the duration of your subscription.

That's the definition. One sentence. Save it for later.

Public proxies, by contrast, are open to anyone. Often unstable. Often already burned by spammers. Shared proxies sit in the middle: cheaper, but split between multiple users. When people search for cheapest private proxies, they're usually trying to land in that sweet spot — exclusive IPs without paying enterprise prices for the privilege.

Here's the unfair stereotype: cheap proxies equal leaky, slow, sketchy. And honestly? Plenty of bargain-bin providers earned that reputation. Bot farms reselling the same IPs across thirty different storefronts. Mystery billing. The kind of thing that gets you a chargeback and a headache.

What separates a trustworthy cheap proxy from a junk one comes down to four things:

- **Network ownership**: Does the provider control its own IP pool, or is it reselling someone else's discount bin?

- **Authentication options**: Username and password plus IP whitelist support, or just one method?
- **Refund or trial policy**: Can you bail within a few days if it doesn't work for your specific use case?

Get those four right and "cheapest" becomes "smartest."
